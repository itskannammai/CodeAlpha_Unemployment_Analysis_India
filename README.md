# Unemployment Analysis in India (2019–2020) – COVID Impact Study

## Project Overview

This project analyzes unemployment trends in India using real-world data.  
The study focuses on understanding the impact of COVID-19 on unemployment rates across regions, areas (Rural vs Urban), and time periods.

The analysis was performed using **Python in Google Colab**.

---

## Objectives

- Analyze unemployment trends over time  
- Identify the impact of COVID-19 on employment  
- Compare Rural vs Urban unemployment  
- Identify states with the highest unemployment rates  
- Study relationships between employment indicators  

---

## Tools & Technologies Used

- Python  
- Pandas  
- NumPy  
- Matplotlib  
- Seaborn  
- Google Colab  

---

## Dataset Information

The dataset contains:

- Region  
- Date  
- Frequency  
- Estimated Unemployment Rate (%)  
- Estimated Employed  
- Estimated Labour Participation Rate (%)  
- Area (Rural / Urban)  

**Total Records:** 768  
**Cleaned Records Used:** 740  

---

## Key Analysis Performed

### 1️. Trend Analysis

- Monthly unemployment trend plotted  
- Sharp spike observed during April 2020 (COVID lockdown period)  

---

### 2️. COVID Impact

- **Pre-COVID Average:** 9.40%  
- **During COVID Average:** 15.17%  
- Significant increase in unemployment due to lockdown  

---

### 3️. Rural vs Urban Comparison

- **Urban Unemployment:** 13.17%  
- **Rural Unemployment:** 10.32%  
- Urban areas were more affected  

---

### 4️. State-Level Analysis

Highest unemployment observed in:

- Bihar (18.92%)  
- Chandigarh (15.99%)  

---

### 5️. Correlation Analysis

- Weak negative relationship between unemployment and employment  
- Labour participation shows minimal correlation  

---

## Visualizations Included

- Line plot of unemployment trend  
- Bar chart comparison (Rural vs Urban)  
- Top 10 states unemployment chart  
- Correlation heatmap  

---

## Key Insights

- COVID-19 significantly disrupted employment patterns.  
- Urban regions experienced greater unemployment impact.  
- Regional disparities exist across states.  
- Economic shocks strongly influence labor market stability.
